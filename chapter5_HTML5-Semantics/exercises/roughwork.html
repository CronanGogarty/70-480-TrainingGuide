The Quickride Steps

Quickride is the CASI-approved method of teaching beginners how to snowboard. It is comprised of the following steps;

  1. Introductions

    A brief introduction is made by the instructor. Then the students should introduce themselves.

    In order to make this step fun, and less daunting to the students, this step can be combined with some warm-up exercises and games.

  2. Equipment

    The instructor will introduce the equipment and explain the role of each item. By the end of this step each student should be able to identify the pieces of equipment and what they are used for.

    Similar to step 1, in order to keep this step fun and stimulating, games and warm-up exercises can be used.

  3. Skating
    • Skating with one foot attached to to board.
    • Walking uphill with one foot attached
    • Walking downhill with one foot attached
    • If time permits - a quick explanation of how to turn the board with one foot attached
  4. Straight-running

    Instructor gives a brief talk on balance, body-position and weight-distribution.

    On a gentle incline, the instroctor will demonstrate how to slide in a very striaght line with front-foot attached and back-foot standing on board.

    • Heel-drag turn (hypno-turn)
    • Toe-drag turn
  5. Edging
  6. Turning
  7. Control
